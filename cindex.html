WORK

Whether you're a freelancer, entrepreneur, telecommuter, or someone who just needs access to an office, OPEN DOOR COWORKING has a spot for you. Coworking is for anyone who needs a desk or a place to meet with clients that isn't a coffee shop or a kitchen table.

COLLABORATE

One of the best things about a coworking space is the diversity of the membership. Coworking is not just for programmers and designers anymore. It's for lawyers, CPAs, photographers, and anyone else. Putting such an assortment of backgrounds into a single area often results in COLLABORATION that may not have otherwise had the chance to happen.

INNOVATE

Putting so many diverse minds together not only increases collaboration, but INNOVATION as well. While members of a coworking space may all be from different companies or have different purposes for being there, opening the lines of communication between different fields opens up endless possibilities of what can come out of it.

Still curious about what a coworking space is?

A Coworking Space is a space that is dedicated to the Coworking concept. These spaces represent a critical foundation of infrastructure for a new and growing workforce of people who work where, when, and how they want. A coworking space's relationship with its members is one that is primarily predicated on the values that drive the Coworking Movement, in direct and deliberate contrast to a more traditional relationship predicated on renting space from a landlord. -- whatiscoworking.com

Open Door Coworking believes that the nine-to-five, fixed office setting is antiquated and stifling to today's workforce. We provide the infrastructure to support this emerging trend in workstyle. Whether you're in the beginning stages of a start-up or have been telecommuting or working from home for decades, we are your office space without the hassle of spending a ton of money on leasing or renting an office or paying utilities.

Use our desks and our chairs, or bring your own. Use our internet and meeting area. Keep your overhead low and concentrate on what matters: your business.

Become a member of the Coworking movement. If you think that renting an entire office or building is too much overhead for your needs or if you aren't sure that renting an office is what you need, become a member of a coworking space and Work. Together.
